# 01 — Visual Identity

## Core idea

This is not a generic developer portfolio. It is a personal data interface built around one idea:

> **Raw data becomes useful when someone gives it structure, meaning, and direction.**

The portfolio presents El Houssaine Ouahad as a Technicien Spécialisé en Intelligence Artificielle — Option Data Analyst who turns messy information into clear dashboards, insights, and practical digital solutions.

## Personality

- Precise, curious, analytical, human, quietly confident.
- Editorial and intelligent, not corporate and not “AI futuristic”.
- Every visual element must communicate hierarchy, clarity, movement, or evidence.

## Visual direction

Use a **dark editorial data interface**: a calm near-black canvas, restrained data-like graphics, strong typography, and warm human details. The site should feel like a designed instrument, not a template dashboard.

## Palette

```text
Ink / background       #0B0F14
Surface                #111820
Elevated surface       #17212B
Primary text           #F4F7F5
Muted text             #91A0AD
Mint signal            #63E6BE
Electric blue          #5B8CFF
Warm evidence          #F4B860
Danger / caution       #FF7A6E
Fine border            rgba(244,247,245,.12)
```

Mint is the primary identity accent. Blue is used for data/navigation. Warm amber is used rarely for human details, milestones, or highlighted results.

## Typography

- Display: **Space Grotesk** or **Geist**, bold and tight.
- Body: **Inter** or **Geist**, calm and highly readable.
- Labels: uppercase, small, tracked, used sparingly.
- Avoid excessive giant headings, italic tech clichés, and all-caps paragraphs.

## Graphic language

- Fine lines inspired by chart axes and data paths.
- Small coordinate labels and index numbers only when useful.
- Subtle grain/noise layer for depth.
- Generous empty space.
- Cards should feel like editorial panels, not glossy glassmorphism.
- Border radius: mostly 12–18px; avoid excessive pill shapes.

## Non-negotiables

- No random gradients, fake statistics, stock photos, robot imagery, or meaningless 3D objects.
- No “AI-generated” wording or inflated claims.
- No animation without a narrative or interaction purpose.
- Never invent projects, clients, metrics, certificates, or job titles.
